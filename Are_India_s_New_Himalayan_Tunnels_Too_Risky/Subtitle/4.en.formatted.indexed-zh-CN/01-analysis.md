## Translation Challenges
1. **Speaker transitions**: `>>` marks TV news soundbites; first-person quotes are from Arnold Dixs. Preserve the spoken/colloquial feel.
2. **Technical precision**: Engineering terms need accurate Chinese equivalents, with original in parentheses on first use.
3. **Spoken fillers**: "uh", "um", "I I", "they they" etc. are natural speech disfluencies — keep them in translated form as they convey authenticity.
4. **Sentence numbering**: Every `[N]` line must be preserved exactly — no merging or splitting.
5. **Chapter headings**: All `#` headings must be translated.
6. **Mixed registers**: Narration is polished; expert quotes are casual/spoken; news anchors are formal. Maintain these distinctions.
