## Content Summary
A documentary about the Tour Montparnasse — Paris's only and controversial skyscraper — covering its history, construction engineering challenges (metro tunnel reinforcement, slip-form construction, deep piling), the public backlash that led to a 1977 building-height ban, and an ongoing renovation plan designed to make the tower "invisible" through transparent glazing.

## Translation Challenges

1. **ASR transcription errors**: The source is auto-transcribed from speech, so many proper nouns are garbled (Tormon Panass = Tour Montparnasse, Osman = Haussmann, Lafon = La Défense, Bazani = Pisani, Jagard Stang = Valéry Giscard d'Estaing). Translate to the correct Chinese rendering of the intended proper noun.
2. **Numbered lines must be preserved exactly**: Each `[N]` prefix must appear on its own line in the output; no merging or splitting of lines.
3. **Chapter headings (#)** must be translated.
4. **Sponsor segment** (lines 30–45): straight Arrow News ad read — translate naturally but keep the promotional tone.
5. **Expert quotes** (lines introduced by >>): slightly more formal register than presenter narration.
6. **Humor and self-aware asides**: e.g., "[snorts]" should remain as "[笑]" or equivalent; "Stop us if you've heard this one before" → conversational Chinese phrasing.
7. **Technical engineering content** (lines 85–110): use precise Chinese civil-engineering terminology.

## Style Guidance
Conversational + storytelling: smooth, engaging Chinese that sounds like a well-produced documentary narration. Expert quotes slightly more formal. Sponsor segment bright and friendly. Maintain the presenter's slightly self-deprecating humor.
