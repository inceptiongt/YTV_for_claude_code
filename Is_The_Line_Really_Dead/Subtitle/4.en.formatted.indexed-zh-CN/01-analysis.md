## 1. Domain

This is a **YouTube documentary/investigative video** from a construction-focused channel (B1N — "the definitive video channel for construction"). The content covers:

- The NEOM megaproject in Saudi Arabia, specifically **The LINE** — a proposed 170 km long linear city
- Construction engineering and technology (foundations, piling, skyscraper cores, mirror cladding, dewatering systems)
- Urban planning theory and transportation infrastructure
- Geopolitical and socioeconomic commentary on Saudi Arabia's Vision 2030 plan
- A **sponsored segment** for AMD Threadripper Pro processors (segments [53]–[63])

The video blends **technical engineering explainer** with **journalistic investigation** and **opinion commentary**, making it a hybrid documentary. It targets an informed general audience, not specialist engineers.

---

## 2. Tone

- **Primary register:** Engaging, accessible popular-science/documentary narration — conversational but substantive
- **Secondary register:** Journalistic investigation with critical commentary; cautious skepticism about NEOM's viability
- **Presenter style:** Direct address to the audience ("I don't know about you", "let's find out", "you guessed it"), occasional humor and rhetorical flourishes
- **Expert quote segments (>> markers):** More formal, measured, and analytical — real estate/development industry professionals speaking candidly
- **Sponsored segment ([53]–[63]):** Promotional/marketing tone, deliberately upbeat
- **Overall pacing:** Fast-paced, punchy sentences. Many short declarative sentences used for dramatic effect (e.g., "[7] Or is it?", "[19] So, will it ever be built?")

In Chinese translation, the narration should feel lively and accessible (偏口语化的科普/纪录片风格), with expert quotes sounding more measured and formal (专业评论腔调).

1. **Preserve all structural markers** exactly: `[N]` index numbers, `>>` speaker markers, and `#` chapter headings.
2. **Correct the source transcription errors** silently (quaternary, NEOM, Gulf of Aqaba, Blade Runner, etc.) — translate the intended meaning, not the garbled text.
3. **Maintain colloquial energy** in the narrator's voice; use lively but not slangy Chinese.
4. **Expert quotes** (marked >>) should sound more measured and analytical.
5. **The sponsor segment** ([53]–[63]) should maintain its promotional, upbeat tone.
6. **Short dramatic sentences** should remain short in Chinese — resist the urge to combine them.
7. **Keep English brand names** (THE LINE, NEOM, AMD, Thread Ripper Pro) in their English form or use official Chinese brand names where they exist.
