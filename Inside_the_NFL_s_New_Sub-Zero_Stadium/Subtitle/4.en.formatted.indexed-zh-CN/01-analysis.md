# Content Analysis

## Domain & Genre
Sports documentary / video essay narration. YouTube channel "The B1M" — specializes in construction and engineering megaprojects. This episode covers the new NFL stadium for the Buffalo Bills (New Highmark/Highark Stadium), examining its architecture, engineering, cost, and fan experience philosophy.

## Tone & Voice
- Conversational, enthusiastic, slightly editorial
- Narrator speaks directly to camera ("you'll," "trust me," "call me cliche")
- Mix of scripted narration and interview quotes (marked with `>>`)
- Occasional humor ("eyewashering," "[snorts]")
- UK English usage throughout (e.g., "across the pond," "kitted out," "gigs")

## Structure
- Chapter headings segment the content (Intro, The NFL is massive, Why build new Highmark, etc.)
- Interview quotes mixed with narration — requires distinct rendering in Chinese
- Sponsor segment (Brilliant) embedded mid-video — keep as-is

## Translation Challenges

1. **Multiple spellings of the stadium name**: "New Hark," "New Highark," "New Highmark," "New High Mark" — all refer to the same stadium. Normalize to "新海马克体育场" with first occurrence noting the English name.
2. **UK vs Chinese idioms**: "across the pond," "kitted out," "gigs," "hearkens back to" — need natural Chinese equivalents.
3. **Numbered format constraint**: `[N]` prefix on every line must be preserved exactly. No merging or splitting.
4. **Interview quotes (`>>`)**:  Keep `>> ` prefix to distinguish speakers.
5. **Sponsor segment**: Brilliant sponsorship content (lines 45–64) — translate naturally but keep brand name "Brilliant" in English.
6. **Technical engineering terms**: hydronic system, perforation wind manipulation, canopy angle for acoustics — use precise Chinese technical language.
7. **Humor and colloquialisms**: "eyewashering" (likely "eyewatering" misspoken), "[snorts]," "rolling in money" — adapt culturally.
8. **Chapter headings (#)**: Translate naturally as section titles.

## Style Guidance
- Conversational, engaging Chinese — as if a knowledgeable friend is explaining a fascinating building project
- Keep sentences punchy and natural; avoid literal translations that feel stilted
- Maintain energy and enthusiasm of the original narration
- For interview quotes, use a slightly more casual register
