# Content Analysis

## Domain & Genre
Engineering/infrastructure documentary subtitle script. The B1M YouTube channel — narrative-driven, educational content about megaprojects.

## Tone & Voice
- Conversational and engaging, with rhetorical questions and direct address ("you might be thinking", "let's take a look")
- Occasional dry humor ("I actually really quite like the look of life in Reunion")
- Dramatic storytelling with tension-building ("that's when they ran into trouble")
- Mix of technical descriptions and human interest

## Translation Challenges
1. **Numbered lines must be kept 1-to-1**: every `[N]` in English → exactly one `[N]` in Chinese; no merging or splitting
2. **`#` chapter headings** must be translated
3. **Conversational asides** (sponsor segment [36-48]) need to maintain the original breezy, self-aware humor
4. **Technical passages** (bridge construction [55-69]) need precision without losing flow
5. **French/island names**: Reunion, Saint-Denis, Le Port, Bois Blanc, Saint-Leu — retain in original + add Chinese in parentheses on first mention
6. **`>>` markers** at start of some lines: preserve as-is
7. **Rhetorical questions** should sound natural in Chinese

## Style Notes
- Style: conversational (as per EXTEND.md)
- Audience: general readers
- Keep short punchy sentences short; don't over-explain
- Maintain the narrator's enthusiastic, slightly breathless energy
