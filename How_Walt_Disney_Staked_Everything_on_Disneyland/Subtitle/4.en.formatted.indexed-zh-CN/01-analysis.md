# Content Analysis

## Domain & Genre
Engineering/architecture history documentary narration. The B1M channel's signature style: infrastructure storytelling blending technical detail with human drama. Target audience: general viewers interested in construction megaprojects.

## Tone & Voice
Conversational yet authoritative. Engaging, slightly dramatic. Short punchy sentences mixed with longer explanatory ones. Occasional rhetorical questions. Direct second-person address ("Do you notice...").

## Structure
10 chapters with [N] indexed subtitle lines (1–119). Chapter titles: Intro / In a rut / Inspiration strikes / The plan / Work begins / Tricks of the trade / Groundbreaking / Getting things ready / More tricks / Opening day / The future.

## Translation Challenges
1. **Numbered indices**: Each `[N]` must map 1-to-1; never merge or split lines
2. **Sentence fragments**: Some lines (e.g., [5], [6]) are split mid-sentence — translate as-is, following the break
3. **Typos in original**: "silvercreen" → 银幕; "bolard" → 护柱; "Joe Fowl" → Joe Fowler
4. **Rhetorical rhythm**: Short emphatic sentences (e.g., "[103] Well, no. The whole thing was a total disaster.") need punch in Chinese
5. **Disney jargon**: "weenies", "imagineers", "Go Away Green" — explain on first occurrence
6. **Promotional ending**: Lines [117]–[119] are a channel plug — translate naturally, keep brand names
7. **Conversational style per EXTEND.md**: Keep it friendly and spoken-like, as if narrating to a friend
