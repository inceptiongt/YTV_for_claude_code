## Domain & Type
Engineering/infrastructure documentary — YouTube video transcript about Switzerland's second Gotthard Road Tunnel. Combines accessible popular-science narration with on-site expert interviews.

## Tone & Voice
- Primary narrator: casual, enthusiastic, slightly irreverent British presenter style (The B1M YouTube channel)
- Expert interviews: non-native English speakers (Swiss engineers), with some grammatical irregularities and stuttering that should be smoothed in Chinese while preserving meaning
- Mid-video book promotion segment: warmly self-promotional, self-deprecating humor
- Overall: conversational, engaging, optimistic about engineering

## Translation Challenges
1. **Numbered format**: Each line begins with `[N]` — these must be preserved exactly as-is, one-to-one correspondence
2. **Chapter headings** `# Heading`: Must be translated (e.g., `# Intro` → `# 开场`)
3. **Expert interview speech**: Grammatically imperfect English from non-native speakers (segments [36]-[38], [79], [89], [96]-[97], [128]-[134], [166]-[168]) — translate the intended meaning smoothly
4. **Self-referential humor**: Preserve the light, self-deprecating tone in the book promotion section
5. **[snorts]**: Sound effect at [106] — translate as `[笑声]` or keep as `[snorts]`
6. **Technical explanations**: Segments explaining TBMs, shotcrete, etc. — keep accessible, not overly technical
7. **Numbers & units**: Keep as-is (57 km, 12 m, 20 million, etc.) — no conversion needed

## Style Notes
- Target: conversational Chinese, as if a knowledgeable friend is explaining engineering to you
- Avoid stiff, formal phrasing — keep it lively and engaging
- Short sentences preferred for subtitle readability
- Colloquial expressions like "sticky situation", "yep, you guessed it" need natural Chinese equivalents
