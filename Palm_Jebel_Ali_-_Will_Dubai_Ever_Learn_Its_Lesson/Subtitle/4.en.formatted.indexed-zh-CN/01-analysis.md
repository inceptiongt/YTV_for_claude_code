## Domain & Type
Engineering documentary / infrastructure journalism — YouTube video narration (The B1M channel)

## Topic Summary
Palm Jebel Ali: Dubai's second massive artificial palm-shaped island project. The video covers Dubai's comeback from the 2008 financial crisis, the construction techniques used for the new island, and whether Dubai has learned from past mistakes.

## Tone & Voice
- Conversational documentary narration — direct address to viewer ("you", "we", "I")
- Mix of enthusiastic journalism and technical explanation
- Slightly cheeky/witty at times ("it is a sequel", "Simple", "rainy old London")
- Sponsored ad segment embedded mid-video (Rayon Design)
- Interview/news clips marked with `>>` prefix

## Structural Notes
- 114 numbered sentences [1]–[114]
- 5 chapter headings: # Intro, # Dubai's first reinvention, # Dubai 2.0, # Building world's biggest palm island… again., # After the crash

## Translation Challenges

1. **Fragmented sentence numbering**: Each [N] line must map exactly to one Chinese [N] line. Do NOT merge or split lines. Some lines end mid-sentence (deliberate subtitle formatting).
2. **Transcript errors**: "Palm Jimra", "Palm Jayra", "Palm Jabali", "Palm Jebali" are all OCR/transcript variants — translate consistently as appropriate character name.
3. **Mid-sentence line breaks**: Lines [7], [8], [9], [18], [19], [33], [34], [36], [37], [38], [39], [44], [45], [63], [64], [65], [66], [67], [85], [86], [87], [88], [93], [94], [102], [103], [108], [109] end mid-sentence — preserve the exact [N] prefix and the partial sentence.
4. **Ad segment**: Lines [40]–[51] are a sponsored segment — translate naturally but don't expand or explain beyond what's said.
5. **News clip quotes**: Lines [22]–[25] use `>>` prefix — keep the `>>` in translation.
6. **Conversational register**: Keep "guys", "honestly", "naturally" etc. in relaxed spoken Chinese style.
7. **Technical accuracy**: Geotechnical terms (vibrocompaction, liquefaction, soil skeleton) need precise engineering Chinese.

## Style Notes
- Style: conversational — friendly, spoken-like, as if explaining to a friend
- Audience: general — plain language, annotate specialized terms where needed
- Keep metric units as-is (km, m, %)
- Keep "$26 billion", "$10 billion" as 260亿美元, 100亿美元
